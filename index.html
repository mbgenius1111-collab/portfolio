Case Study 01 · Orthopedic Surgery

3-Physician Orthopedic Group — Texas

A three-physician orthopedic group in Texas with heavy surgical billing volume was experiencing a 34% denial rate on surgical claims — nearly triple the acceptable benchmark. Appeals were rarely filed. Revenue was eroding month over month.

Root Causes Identified
  • 34% first-pass denial rate on surgical claims — primary issue
  • Mismatched CPT/diagnosis code combinations across 6 recurring code sets
  • Modifier errors — incorrect or missing modifiers on bilateral and add-on procedures
  • Appeals almost never filed after initial denials
  • No pre-authorization tracking system for elective surgical procedures
MBC Intervention
  • Full billing audit — 6 recurring coding errors identified and corrected
  • Surgical notes reviewed before coding on all new submissions
  • Corrected claims and appeals filed for 90 days of denied claims
  • Pre-authorization tracking system implemented for elective procedures

Case Study 02 · Internal Medicine

Solo Internal Medicine Physician — Florida

A solo internal medicine physician was struggling with 65 days in AR, a 40% patient collection rate, and underpayments from contracted payers that had gone undetected for months. Cash flow was unstable despite a healthy patient volume.

Root Causes Identified
  • No real-time eligibility verification at front desk — deductibles and copays unknown at visit
  • No protocol to collect patient balances at point of service
  • 2 major contracted payers consistently reimbursing below contractual rate
  • No payer reimbursement analysis had ever been performed
MBC Intervention

  • Ran payer reimbursement analysis — identified 2 under-paying payers

Case Study 03 · Behavioral Health

6-Provider BH Group Practice — Pennsylvania

A group practice with 6 licensed therapists (2 LCSWs, 3 LPCs, 1 Psychiatrist) billing approximately 1,100 claims/month. Despite solid patient volume, a 31% first-pass denial rate was costing the practice an estimated $22,400 in lost revenue every single month.

Root Causes Identified
  • Modifier 95 (telehealth) missing on 38% of virtual session claims
  • ICD-10 codes not updated after DSM-5-TR revision — outdated F-codes triggering auto-review flags
  • One LCSW's NPI not credentialed with 2 major payers — 4 months of claims auto-denied
  • Group therapy claims (90853) submitted without required 'HQ' modifier — 100% denial rate
  • Session limit tracking spreadsheet not synced with EHR — 90 claims filed after annual cap
MBC Intervention
  • Full 90-day retrospective audit — corrected claims submitted with proper documentation
  • Fee schedule rebuilt in PM system with accurate CPT-modifier mapping
  • Emergency credentialing for LCSW with 2 payers — 4 months of backlogged claims recovered
  • Automated eligibility verification implemented 48 hours before every appointment
  • Staff trained on documentation requirements that directly impact billing outcomes

"Behavioral health billing isn't broken because the codes are hard. It's broken because practices treat it like regular medical billing. It isn't."

— Muhammad Bilawal, Founder & RCM Consultant, MBC RCM

After months auditing BH practices, MBC has identified six recurring challenges that silently drain revenue — none of which show up in a standard billing report until it's too late.

Medical Necessity Scrutiny
Payers demand detailed clinical documentation to justify every session — far more than most medical specialties require.
Frequent Policy Changes
Coverage rules for telehealth BH, group therapy, and crisis services change multiple times per year — most practices miss them.
Credentialing Gaps
LCSWs, LPCs, and NPs often face mid-cycle credentialing issues that silently block reimbursement for months.
Diagnosis Code Sensitivity
Wrong ICD-10 codes can flag a claim for pre-authorization or automatic denial without any notice to the practice.
Session Limit Tracking
Most payers impose annual session caps. Manual tracking is where money disappears — claims submitted after cap = 100% denial.
POS Code Errors
Telehealth POS 02 vs 10 mismatches are among the top rejection triggers since 2021 — invisible until the EOB arrives.
